Step 1: The Demographic Landscape

Understanding the raw distribution of our customer base before applying machine learning optimization.

Age & Spending Distribution

Analysis

The distribution reveals a significant concentration of customers in their late 20s and early 30s. This young-adult demographic represents the most active mall participants, showing a strong correlation between age and mall engagement (Spending Score).

Income vs. Spending

Analysis

Early visualization shows natural "pockets" of data points. Specifically, we see clusters forming at the high-income/low-spending and high-income/high-spending extremes, hinting at distinct psychological profiles.

Step 2: Optimization via K-Means

Using the Elbow Method to mathematically determine the most efficient number of segments.

The Elbow Method

To find the optimal 'K' (number of clusters), we calculate the Sum of Squared Errors (Inertia) for various cluster counts.

  • Initial Drop: Significant decrease from K=1 to K=4.
  • The Elbow: The rate of decrease plateaus significantly at K=5.
  • Conclusion: 5 clusters provide the best balance between granularity and model simplicity, as used in the initial study.
